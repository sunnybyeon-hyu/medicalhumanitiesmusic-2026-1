# Miracle in Cell No. 7

The plot of *Miracle in Cell No. 7* begins when Yong-gu, a father with an intellectual disability, lives happily with his young daughter Ye-seung but is falsely accused of a crime and sent to prison. At first, the inmates in Cell No. 7 are cold and distant toward him, but they gradually come to understand his innocence and sincere love for his daughter, eventually becoming like a family. In particular, the song “Do You Know” is used during emotional scenes in the film, emphasizing the deep bond and longing between Yong-gu and Ye-seung. Through this process, the inmates begin to accept Yong-gu not simply as someone who needs protection, but as a person with whom they can laugh and empathize. The song conveys a sense of sorrow and warmth through its emotional melody and lyrical atmosphere, helping the audience feel closer to Yong-gu and understand him as a human being rather than seeing him only as a tragic or vulnerable figure. In addition, the discrimination shown throughout the false accusation and trial process suggests that social prejudice and institutional injustice are greater problems than disability itself, encouraging viewers to reflect on these issues more deeply.
